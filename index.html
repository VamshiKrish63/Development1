Existing System

Sand import and export involve the global trade of sand, primarily for construction and land reclamation. Countries like Singapore import vast quantities due to limited local resources, while nations with abundant sand, such as Malaysia and Indonesia, export it. This trade is driven by construction booms and urbanization. However, environmental concerns arise from over-extraction, leading to habitat destruction and erosion. Regulations and bans are increasingly common to address these issues. The sand trade reflects the complex balance between development needs and environmental sustainability.
